<p><p>The communication cables market consists of three primary types: copper, aluminum, and aluminum alloy cables. Copper cables are widely used due to their excellent conductivity and reliability, making them ideal for high-speed data transmission. Aluminum cables, although lighter and more cost-effective, offer lower conductivity and are often used in applications where weight is a concern. Aluminum alloy cables combine the benefits of both materials, providing enhanced strength and conductivity, making them suitable for a variety of communication needs.</p></p>
