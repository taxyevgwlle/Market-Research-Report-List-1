<p><p>The dry yeast market comprises two primary types: active dry yeast and inactive dry yeast. Active dry yeast is a living organism used in baking and brewing, requiring rehydration before use to ferment sugars and produce carbon dioxide, which helps dough rise. In contrast, inactive dry yeast is not viable for fermentation but serves as a nutritional supplement, offering benefits such as vitamins and minerals. Both types cater to distinct segments of the food and beverage industry, fulfilling diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
